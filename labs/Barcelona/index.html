Real final project: Nighty lighty

To begin with, I can only say that my Gossip Goblins will have to be postponed. This time, the character will be Nighty Lighty. But, what on earth is Nighty Lighty?! Well, the origin of this friendly pet is Biggy, one of the results of the ‘make something big’ assignment. Biggy was a press-fit car that kids could use in order to put their toys; what’s more, it had some moving parts thanks to this kind of ’sandwich’ between 5 components: 2 fixed (used as walls), the central piece that grabs the mobile, the mobile, and the transverse one. The interesting thing about this mechanism is that it doesn’t need nails, glue, or bolt…it is just..press-fit! The image below shows some things made using it.

I am really interested in making things for kids, so I opted to research and look for all the wooden, plastic, and metal cars for kids. Besides the toys-storage function, it would be good if the kids use it as a vehicle. About what I found, I realized that many of this vehicles don’t have many electronic parts. If Iwere a kid, I would like to have a vehicle to use during the day and at night. It would be good if the kid had the option to ride it during the night (probably with his/her parents), and it order to get it, it is necessary to have light. Below, you can see all the existing products. They look incredible!

As you can see, they have very nice colors and shapes. They are used for storage, and also for transportation. Some of them go to the front, and others can turn because of the steering. These cars are appropriate for the development of the kids, since thay help them walk, they help to avoid mess, kids can have fun using it, kids must learn to control their strenght (hands, legs), and they must have their eyes open (unless the kids like to crash on the wall..).

Nighty Lighty’s users are kids from 1 to 5 years old. The goal of this new pet is to help the kid develop the gross motor skills (walk, run, move, put objets inside/outside, grab), to experiment some principles of electronics (light sensor activates LEDs), to share its time with parents so they would help to assemble (the idea is to make Nighty Lighty a press-fit car with different pieces, like head, and other parts), and to have fun. The material used is plywood, and some PCBs use light sensor as input and some LEDs as output (I am still thinking about the location of the items). The sketch of Nighty Lighty is the following…

I know the monster looks really monster, but its shape must change according to the measure of the kid. This means, ergonomics will play an important role for this monster.It shouldn’t be too high or to small, the width of the kid must fit inside Nighty Lighty, and as mentioned before, the location of LEDs, sensor and battery is the most important part of this. Somehow, I must hide them. In order to put the battery inside, the options are to cover it with the press fit or make a small cage with the molding and casting technique.

Is is bad to see that we have only 3 weeks left. I don’t recommend anyone to do this the last month of classes, since you never know what could happen. Finally, the calendar is the one below:

Today, it is Wednesday 12th, May and the final day is June the 2nd. I hope the calendar is understood. The steps are: Documentation process, Design/Files/Render, Fabrication/Assembling test, Electronics, Assembling electronics/plywood, Possible changes, and Final presentation. Each one has a different color, and they must be accomplished during the colored dates strictly! It is important to measure things well to avoid mistakes, so the ‘Possible changes’ step will not exist! For the documentation process, I will gather each weekend what has been done, through videos, pictures and any other source. The tools used are shopbot, laser cutter , and MDX-20. About classes, I am not sure yet whether I will need of molding and casting for the battery. To sum up, let’s continue with Nighty Lighty…and the plan for distributing it!

Sensors, Actuators and Displays

For this assignment, I tried to make an input/output interface where the light sensor controls the RGB. This one is quite similar to previous assignments from other people since instead of using a thermistor, I used the light sensor. I also tried to modify it changing the MTA connector by the new ones.

In this case, first it is necessary to connect a 9V battery in order to have the circuit working. When testing the board (and as you will see in the video) without light in the place, the RGB will go blue. When there is some light, it will go green, and when there is too much light, the RGB will go red. Now, in the assembler code, you will see that as outputs we have PB1 for BLUE, PB4 for GREEN, and PB3 for RED. It is interesting to see that the sequence of turning-on will change if the relation is different. I mean, PB1 could be RED, and PB3 could be blue. Anoter important fact is that they are defined as outputs in ’sbi PORTB’ and ’sbi DDRB’.

You can see the result of this experiment below:

Then, analysing the circuit, I realized that the place, where the light sensor is (see the orange line in the previous image), could be modified by any other component that behaves as a resistor (my friend Beno Juarez is proposing a universal connector for this area); therefore, two pieces of copper (like for the step response) could be considered an input, and the output, instead of being the RGB (see the red, blue, and green lines) could be LEDs (where letter ‘a’ is the voltage). For the following experiment, I opted to use only two LEDs (blue and red lines). First, check the anodes and cathodes of the LEDs. Then, the result is that when the copper sheets are separate, one LED is on, and the other one OFF, whereas when both sheets are together the LEDs change.

Longitudinal effectThe amount of charge produced is strictly proportional to the applied force and is independent of size and shape of the piezoelectric element. Using several elements that are mechanically in series and electrically in parallel is the only way to increase the charge output. The resulting charge is
Cx = dxxFxn, where dxx is the piezoelectric coefficient for a charge in x-direction released by forces applied along x-direction (in pC/N). Fx is the applied Force in x-direction [N] and n corresponds to the number of stacked elements .

Final Project… SMALL Intelligent Dobby-Loom

For my final project, I want to build a weaving machine which can read designs from computer, change different color wefts. Basically a dobby loom works in such a way that the design which comes out on the garment is fed through a mechanically programmable set of pins on a wheel on top/or side/ of the loom(body). The warp and weft are coordinated to make a design on the final garment.

The ghosted View shows the Four motors taking care of the Four Harnesses to be lifted and dropped for every insertion of Weft in to warps

One can make the variety in colors or thread thickness in the WARP or in the WEFT or in BOTH. But most easy way to do this is making the variance in the WEFT only.

What I want my machine to do is to change the weft according to the data given for it through a computer. This may share some Ideas from Modela Circuit maker, taking the cad file and proceeding to cut. I have the mechanical model of the machine below and I think working on the electronic part may be a big challenge.
