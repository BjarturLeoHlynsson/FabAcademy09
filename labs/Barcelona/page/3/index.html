For this new assignment, I really had trouble. To begin with, I decided to make many of the sensors, like the step response, the one for temperature, the light sensor, and microphone. About the microphone, the first time it didn’t work. After analysing some minutes, I noticed that the bit didn’t machine all it should. There was a path where the bit didn’t go trough. What’s more, I am still looking for more and more information about programming. I think it may be really easy to connect the input and output…but it’s important for me to collect more theory about it to understand it properly.

I WILL learn this…but need more time.

I. 3D scanning

scanning using picza-modella

The aim of this project for 3D scanning assignment is to study the complex form of nature and capture it with 3D printer. I wanted to make something which can not be easily modeled in 3D softwares.  So for this I found the natural objects very interesting.
The machine doesn’t read the bottom part of any scanning object, so i stick it with silicon in-order not to be shaken while on the scanning process.

For this reason it would be better to choose top and side views which I chose is more like conical. I actually scanned two shells, but this one is more convenient for the project.
The things I needed to consider while sending to the machine model la is the maximum and minumum height,maximum and maximum and width, the tool diameter, the x,y,z position on the modella board. After setting all this on picza I started scanning., Since the shell I used to scan was so small the scanning process was not so long, which is 2 hours and 15 minutes.

After finishing the 3D scanning on picza, I export it as STL file. Then on 3D max studio I opened the STL file I saved but not import. I tried to fill the scanned form by editable poly conversion and extruding the below part, I fuse it and try to see the different views and make various
exercises and choose 3/4 for the final one.

I focus on the forms and textures, because besides stl files doesn’t read colors, the project is all about-3D scanning and printings. Scanning with Liquids and Preparing for 3D print

Material: objeto a escanéar, liquido (leche, vinagre, cocacola, o agua con un tinte negro) , camera, recipiente y algún tipo de iluminación, ya que el objeto no puede tener sombras si no puede perder la forma real.
El proceso es muy simple, se sumerge el objeto o partes de el en el liquido que genera contorno del objeto. El nivel de líquidos tiene que ir subiendo proporcionalmente (1mm ~ 1cm) siempre dependiendo del detalle que queramos obtener.
Proceso a seguir:
Preparación:
  1. Preparar el escenario
  2. Tomar fotos desde (Top View)
  3. Vectorización y conversión  al formato .dxf (.cad) 3-1. Retoque de las foto PhotoShop Cs4 (script) 3-2. Vector Magic (http://vectormagic.com/). El Vector Magic nos crea los vectores de forma automática.
  4. Importar archivos en cualquier soft 3D
  5. Separar las capas una de otra, modificar la coordenada correspondiente ”Z”

Milk scaned layers and vectors from Anthony B. on Vimeo.

Modelado 3D con Max 2009 o 2010: Antes de nada unimos todas las capas en una haciendo un (attach) y lo convertimos en editablePoly. Los vértices nos servirán para crear una malla aproximada (en este modelo no hace falta seguir ningún patrón de malla). Separamos los Polygonos creados del objeto y ocultamos el objeto anterior ya que los vértices ya no los vamos a necesitar.

Plugin:

Max 2009) En todas las versiones anteriores a Max 2010 vamos a necesitar plugin llamado PolyBoost (http://www.polyboost.com).

Max 2010) ¿Porque en esta versión no nos hace falta dicho plugin? R:En Max2010 ya lo tiene incluido y lo podemos encontrar en los subTabs situados en la parte superior justo por de-abajo  del menú Poly > PolyDraw. Este plugin nos amplia los métodos del modelado. Lo usaremos para crear una malla correcta a partir del objeto creado desde vértices.

WorkShop (Carlos Castro Prieto)
Escaneamos una mascara de Gas de un tamaño aproximado de 40 x 20cm, que fue sumergido en agua con tinte negro, sobre una superficie solida e en una cubeta de plástico. Para la iluminación básica usamos un foco de 200w – 400w situado por encima del objeto. Para las fotos usamos una camera profesional Canon EOS 400D DIGITAL con una lente de 51mm. En total se tomaron 97 fotos de alta resolución. La separación de entre capas fue de 1mm.

Preparación del modelo para Stereolithography (3D Print):

Modelo debe presentar una malla triangular (en max podemos usar un modificador llamado “subDivide”) que nos transforma nuestra malla en una malla triangular, el modelo debería presentar las dos caras externa y interna. las paredes deberían de ser de un grosor de unos 2mm como mínimo. Todos los splines deberían de estar cerrados ya que puede presentar algún error en la impresión. Y final mente exportamos modelo con un formato “.STL” , “.3Ds” o bien “.OBJ”. Study and modelling of complex forms (molluscs) using Scanner 3D, laser cutter and 3D printer

Objetives: 1. To scan and to rebuild complex 3D structures. 2. To obtain the information stored in the natural forms with a great level of precision. 3. To learn on its structural, its standard of growth and their adaptation to the environment. 4. To design new adaptable structures.

THE PROCESS We experimented in three differents leves :  Conceptual modelling: brainstormings, interaction with the context, internet reference, etc. to define the power idea to make! / Bit´s modelling: after the first idea, we represented it in differents softwares: Modela scanning (hardaware) + Dr. Picza and Rhinoceros.

1. CONCEPTUAL  MODELLING


The natural forms are the result of a constant adaptation to the environment. These structures contain information on  an evolutionary processes that evidence in matter, through the use of the scanner 3D we can obtain  thisinformation and understand with more accurately its standards of structures and use them to new designs.

MUSSEL : The common name mussel is used for members of several families of clams or bivalvia mollusca, from saltwater and freshwater habitats. These groups have in common a shell whose outline is elongated and asymmetrical compared with other edible clams, which are often more or less rounded or oval.

3d Scanning and Printing

The new assignment is related to 3d scanning and printing. To begin with, I’d like to say that this post is a brief of what you will see below from Slideshare. There are some technical things that you should find out before scanning. First, you will need the MDX-20 , that is a scanning/milling machine, and you will also have to install the Roland Software Package. For this exercise (scanning), you will use Dr. Picza software. Be sure to have it in your laptop or your PC.

1  (USB/Serial converter and connection between computer and MDX-20)

Since my laptop doesn’t have serial port, I had to use a USB serial converter; what’s more, I had to download a driver which can be found either looking for it in the Internet or when connecting the converter, so the laptop will be looking for it and install it automatically. After that, it’s necessary to assign a port to the MDX-20. I worked with port COM1. In order to do it, follow this:

Start (bottom left corner of your computer) > Control panel > Classic view > System > Device Manager > Ports (COM & LPT) > USB Serial Port (it shows which one is being used) > right click > Properties > Port settings > Advanced > COM Port Number > I chose COM 1

When you open Dr. Picza, you also have do the following:  File > Preferences > choose COM1

If you have trouble, and the connection is not recognized, try to put the USB Serial converter in other USB  port. It worked for me.

2 (Scanning the object and settings)

First, you must put the object (in my case,  pliers) on the base of the Modela. Stick it well, and be careful with the material you choose. Avoid rubber or fabrics, use something rigid and with low friction instead. Then, let’s star with 1 (controller). When you click on it, a window will open (2). Set the X and Y scan pitch (the less value, the more details, and the more time  it will take). Z bottom is the distance between the base of the Modela and the lowest point the needle will reach.

When you click on Scanning Area a new window will appear (3). The blue rectangle is the area you want, which can be moved manually or using the arrows in (3a). You can also double click on the small squares, so that the needle will go there. This lets you know the smallest area you will need to scan all your object. Then, check on Z upper limit (3b). This is very useful not only because it helps you the set the top surface, the highest point of your object, but it also helps to decrease the scanning time. Finally, click on OK, and SCAN.

3 (Exporting the file)

Above, you can see the pliers, and next to it the scanning result from Dr. Picza. In order to modify its shape, you have to export to another software. Click on File > Export > choose a format. The options were .iges, .stl, and .dxf. If you see the .iges, you can notice that the surface doesn’t look good. In case you want it, you would have to export it with more triangles and the file would be bigger. A good option is .stl. When you open it in Rhinoceros, you will see how good the scanning was. Shapes, textures, imperfections, everything can be seen.

4  (Modifying the object)

When you open it in Rhinoceros, it would be good to save it as .3ds and open it again. From 1 mesh, you will find more meshes, which is easier to control. For printing, the surface must be closed, otherwise you will have mistakes and nothing will be printed. Remember, even a sheet of paper has a thickness! Once you have the meshes, you can create edge lines from the surfaces (1), trim them, join them, extrude them, explode them, and you will have one surface used for covering. The other option is to make a mirror of it and join it. I used the 2nd option. Then, you will be able to modify the object. Type ‘_CageEdit‘, select the object, select the kind of control you want (I chose a box), set its limits (origin, end, height), and you will get (2). Then, decide how many control points you want in X, Y, and Z, and choose to edilt All or Local. I chose All so I can modify the entire object. You will have something like (3).

5 (Creating the character: 3d.Mask)

First draw the character you want (1). Take a picture of it and open it inRhyno. Since I wanted to use a battery, a LED, and a switch, I had to consider and measure everything so they would fit. In (2), you have to draw lines and create surfaces from it. It might take some time to be finished. Besides, the object must have a thickness, so I made an offset of surface of 4mm. You see that the black layer is the interior, and the red one, the exterior. In (3), you see all the surfaces finished. The interior and exterior surfaces from the body, and the mask head. I had to join them to make of them 2 objects: the body, and the mask head. In (4), I created some spheres that would be the acne and joined to the surface with boolean (Solid > Union). To let the LED light come out, we need holes, so first I draw circles of 1 or 2mm diameter, extrude them, and then, I put them wherever Iwant. Then we use the boolean difference  (Solid > Difference). In (5), it is crutial to know whether there are holes in any part of the surface. With this, you are almost ready. To check where the lines are, go to Analysis > Edge tools >Show edges > Naked edges. You will see them in color. Have your time to modify and fix it. Finally, in (6) you have the 3 closed surfaces ready to be exported. Click on File > Export selected > choose the objects > choose .stl > select binary > no check in ‘export open objetcs’ > accept. No message should appear! That would mean it is good.

6 (Printing)

The truth is that I’m not sure about what to do in order to send from the computer to the printer. It had to be scaled, and it was not completely printed. The machine used is ZCorp Z510 3Dprinter. In (1), you open it, in (2) you take out the powder carefully with a brush. In (3) you put your objects in the Powder Recycling Station, and use compressed air to take ALL the powder from the object. Again, be careful with the strength of your hands! In(5), don’t forget to close the door. That powder is not healthy, and makes everything dirty. Finally, in (6), spray it with water, so it will harden your work and make it last more days!

So, that’s the final result. I really enjoyed designing my character and scanning the pliers. It takes many hours, but the fact of seeing something you create gets real is amazing. It’s sad the the 3dprinter had a problem. That’s why I only had 3/4 of 3D.Mask.

For more detailed information about the whole experience and more useful graphics, have a look at the slide presentation. It is kind of long, but it is because there are many steps to consider when doing this work. See you!!!

3D Printing Tutorial

3D Printing using Z Corp z510 at Fab Lab Barcelona. Only monochrome (STL)

ZCorp z510 3D printer

Bed size: 254 x 356 x 203 mm
Layer Thickness: User selectable at the time of printing: .089 – .203 mm
Material: High performance composite, elastomeric, direct casting

The ZCorp 3D printer uses ZPrint to interface with files and the machine. It can import:

- .stl
- .wrml
- .3ds

File preparation:

- Make sure that your model is a SOLID.
- If you are using surfaces, like walls of a building or the skin of a void element you should give a minimum THICKNESS of 2mm, otherwise it will break apart into the 3D printer.
- Check the normals of your model, they should be facing “out” of the part.
- Once you finish modeling your part, export it as a .STL (stereolithography) file.

From Rhino:

There are different commands to check your 3D model for the 3D printer:

- Edit your model by using both Surface or Mesh edit tools in the tool bar.
- Join your meshes/surfaces as one solid object.
- Check naked edges at edge tools > show edges > naked edges
- Check the normals:
- Dir, check the direction of your normals and edit them by Flipping the direction
- UnifyMeshNormals, face your normals in the same direction.

From Daniel Cardoso at MIT:

“Most times when Rhino says that the object is not valid for rapid prototyping is
because the object is not a solid. You can make sure that the object is a solid
in Rhino 4 by checking that it has no naked edges (you can do this by analyze
-> edge tools -> show edges : show naked edges)

This will show you the edges and you can then edit them manually/delete them.”
HMAA 08

“If using Rhino 3.0 or 4.0, there are many ways to modify your model.
Below are some basic commands for modification operations as well as
preparing your model for printing. You can just type these commands

http://en.wiki.mcneel.com/default.aspx/McNeel/CommandList.html
