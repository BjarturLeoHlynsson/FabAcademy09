FAB Academy Self reproducing machines




    1. First thoughts

This first assignment is immediately a huge challenge and touches upon the essence of the FabLab vision. In a first attempt to reflect on a design of a self reproducing machine my thoughts go to:

  • computer viruses

  • living creatures (like cells, animals, and humans)

  • and last with less emphasis to robots


What a difficult assignment to design something that can re-create itself and possible other objects. Already reproducing itself is a challenge.


    2. Conditions

I agree with the statements in “On models of reproduction” by Homer Jacobson for the conditions to be able to create self-reproducing machines/beings. Put in my own words, you need:

  • resources or building material to create elements or parts

  • energy to transform the building material and put together the parts


The system can be put together in a systematic way or in a random way. In case of a random creation process, the building blocks need to have the property to contain a hard coded design of the eventual system such that no mistakes can be made. If you want your self reproducing machine to evolve and adapt to changing circumstances, you might allow deviations in the construction and permit less constraints on how hard coded the design is integrated in building blocks. This is very much according to Darwin's evolution theory: mistakes won't survive, but improvements will. However the rate of creation must be balanced with the rate of losses and the life expectancy of it's creator.


    3. An artificial machine: a Robot

To do this in an artificial machine, you need to create a kind of robot that has a building plan of it self contained in its memory. It also needs to know where to find the resources (materials) it is made of and need to be able to recognize them. Then he needs the tools (e.g. cutter, welding iron etc.) to make building blocks and put them together. For this it needs energy sources. Ideally, it is capable to allocate or transform energy for it's needs. Finally to complete it's own creation it needs to transfer the knowledge (data) including the building plan. Optionally, the machine should be able to repair itself if it gets damaged. For this function in needs incorporated sensors to detect fails or damage.


    4. A living object: A tree house

An additional design idea, and inspired by Drew Endy, I would very much like to see a plant or tree that during it's growth creates a house. The modified DNA of this plant will contain a building plan of the house. Important is that the plant will also grow seeds such that it can reproduce itself.
